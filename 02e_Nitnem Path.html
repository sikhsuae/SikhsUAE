Tav Prasad means with the grace of God. This composition vehemently condemns idolatry, pilgrimages, grave worship, yogi samadhis, and other ceremonial beliefs of Hinduism, Jainism, and Islam as being useless in achieving God is carried out in the absence of love for God and all his creation

Chaupai Sahib

Chaupai Sahib is a significant Sikh prayer (Bani) believed to offer protection and spiritual security. It is one of the five daily prayers (Nitnem) recited by Sikhs and is also incorporated into the evening prayer, Rehras Sahib. Reciting Chaupai Sahib is thought to dispel negativity, instill self-confidence, and provide a sense of reliance on God.

Anand Sahib

Anand Sahib is a significant Sikh prayer (bani) that emphasizes the pursuit of bliss and the path to a joyful life, as outlined by Guru Amar Das, the third Sikh Guru. Its relevance lies in its role as a guide to spiritual and personal development, fostering a sense of well-being and contentment through its verses. Anand Sahib is recited in various Sikh ceremonies and daily prayers, highlighting its central place in Sikh rituals and devotional practice.

Evening Rehras Sahib

Rehras Sahib is the evening prayer in Sikhism, holding significant relevance for its role in connecting Sikhs with their faith, providing spiritual solace, and reinforcing positive values. It is recited after the day's work, offering a chance to reflect, express gratitude, and seek divine support before retiring for the night.

Night Kirtan Sohila

Kirtan Sohila is a Sikh night-time prayer, traditionally recited before sleep, known for its soothing and protective qualities. It's a collection of five hymns (shabads) composed by three Sikh Gurus: Guru Nanak, Guru Ram Das, and Guru Arjan. The prayer's relevance lies in its ability to calm the mind, promote restful sleep, and offer a sense of spiritual protection.

Ardaas

Ardaas is a significant Sikh prayer that serves as a supplication to the divine, seeking blessings, guidance, and strength. It's more than a ritual; it's a deeply personal and communal act of faith, remembrance, and surrender to the will of God. The prayer is recited in various Sikh ceremonies and also before undertaking important tasks or journeys, emphasizing its relevance in both individual and collective life.
